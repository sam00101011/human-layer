# Expansions

## Core principle

The strongest expansions are the ones that make Human Layer feel less like a comments tool and more like human infrastructure for the web.

The product should grow from:
- comments on pages
- search by human signal

into:
- discovery
- reputation
- feeds
- moderation
- trust infrastructure
- platform and enterprise tooling

## Highest-leverage expansion directions

### 1. Human Graph

Turn Human Layer from a page utility into a discovery network.

Key ideas:
- follow people, not platforms
- suggested humans based on interests and platform overlap
- lists and circles
- high-signal people discovery by topic

Why it matters:
- creates network effects
- gives profiles real utility
- makes the product sticky even when a user is not on a specific page

### 2. Human Reputation

Build portable, pseudonymous trust.

Key ideas:
- expertise by topic and domain
- useful-rate
- trust score
- moderation history
- endorsement trails

Why it matters:
- helps users decide whose signals to trust
- makes profiles worth following
- improves search and recommendation quality

### 3. Human Search 2.0

Push search past annotated-page lookup.

Key ideas:
- saved searches
- alerts and digests
- "what verified humans found this week"
- domain- or topic-specific leaderboards
- query expansions based on human tags and summaries

Why it matters:
- turns the product into a repeat destination
- creates daily or weekly habit loops
- gives a reason to come back even without browsing a supported page

### 4. Human Mode

Make the product feel like a browsing behavior, not just an overlay.

Key ideas:
- hide ads
- hide native comments
- show only verified-human summaries, verdicts, and discussion
- default clean reading mode with human context

Why it matters:
- makes the value immediate
- differentiates from a normal extension
- supports a later full browser product

### 5. Human Feeds

Give people a reason to browse inside Human Layer.

Key ideas:
- topic feeds
- domain feeds
- human-only trending
- "top links in Base this week"
- "best AI docs this month"
- "most trusted humans in devtools"

Why it matters:
- creates habit
- complements search
- helps discovery scale beyond direct queries

## More product surfaces

### Object-level threads

Do not limit the system to whole pages only.

Possible objects:
- GitHub repos
- pull requests
- issues
- docs pages
- YouTube videos
- products
- papers
- wallet addresses
- tokens
- founders
- apps

### Human summaries

Every object can have:
- best summary
- best critique
- best "what matters here"
- best beginner explanation
- best "skip this" explanation

### Human verdicts

Useful standard verdicts:
- useful
- misleading
- scam
- outdated
- beginner-friendly
- advanced
- worth-following
- high-signal

### Human lists

Examples:
- best devtools people
- best Base researchers
- best HN builders
- best crypto explainers
- best AI agent evaluators

### Human receipts

A profile should show why someone is worth following:
- what they saved
- what they annotated
- what they endorsed
- where they were found helpful
- where they were disputed

## Social and network expansions

- follow graph
- mutual-interest discovery
- invite-only circles
- per-topic rooms
- one-human juries for moderation
- reputation-weighted endorsements
- portable identity across sites

## Recommended next expansions

If choosing the next three after the current roadmap, prioritize:
1. Human Graph
2. Human Feeds
3. Publisher and trust API

That combination gives:
- consumer network effects
- repeat engagement
- a business model
